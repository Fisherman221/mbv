EP's 1988–1991

Bilinda Butcher

EP's 1988–1991 is an EP compilation by Irish rock band My Bloody Valentine. Released on 4 May 2012 by Domino Records, the EP includes the band's EPs for Creation Records—You Made Me Realise (1988), Feed Me with Your Kiss (1988), Glider (1990) and Tremolo (1991)—and an alternate mix of "Soon".

Compiled to be released by the band's record label and then guitarist Kevin Shields, the 4 May 2012 release was the first new CD release from the band in over a decade. My Bloody Valentine released the two disc album, Isn't Anything (1988) and Loveless (1991) to 4 May 2012. The following EP was the first and largest musical release in the United Kingdom, Belgium, South Africa and Japan.

Background

In 1988, My Bloody Valentine signed with Creation Records and released their first EP, You Made Me Realise, which was followed up by Feed Me with Your Kiss, released on 31 October 1988. In 1990, the band released their third EP, Glider, under the UK music press, then released their fourth EP, Tremolo, in 1991. Although the EP's were successful in the UK, My Bloody Valentine was considered a minor band in the United States. In 1991, at the time of the release of Loveless, My Bloody Valentine was dropped by Creation Records as they found Kevin Shields too difficult to work with. It was at this time that the band was dropped from their contract. It is said that in the late 1980s and early 1990s, when the band were touring the UK, they were constantly touring, which led to a burn out at their main music sources was released.
