you made me realise (ep)

"You Made Me Realise" (stylised in lower case) is the third extended play by the Irish rock band My Bloody Valentine, released on 8 August 1988 on Creation Records. After the poorly received mini-album Ecstasy, the band were invited by Alan McGee to record an EP for his independent record label. The EP was recorded over a week in mid-1988 and features vocals and guitars by Shields and newly recruited Bilinda Butcher, who had no previous musical experience. It is notable for its chaotic, distorted sound and breathless vocals, a style that laid the foundation for the independent music scene in My Bloody Valentine establishing their sound. The single/EP was met with critical acclaim and achieved a number two peak on the UK Independent Singles Chart. The success of the record prompted the band to remain closely affiliated with Creation through the late 1980s and early 1990s.

Recording

recording

From 1985 to 1988, My Bloody Valentine underwent a major line-up change, relocating to London and eventually signing to Creation. [1] [2] The band's lead vocalist Dave Conway departed the band in 1987 after becoming "disillusioned with music". [3] Butcher, who had no previous musical experience, was recruited after answering a NME advertisement for a singer. [4]

Following Conway's departure, the band recorded their final release for Lazy Records, the Strawberry Wine single and Ecstasy mini-album in 1987. Both releases were met with critical panning, although Butcher was praised for her vocal performance and her presence in the band. [5] The band subsequently left Lazy Records in 1988, stating "they never paid us a penny".

In early 1988, the band was noticed by record executive Alan McGee while performing in Canterbury. McGee offered them a recording contract to his label Creation. [6] The band was allotted one week to record the EP. During the sessions, the band recorded five tracks. The recording was completed in a residential studio in London, which the band felt was a chaotic experience due to drug use. [7]

Music

the gear in 1988

You Made Me Realise is a shoegaze record that blends elements of noise pop and dream pop, marking a significant departure from the band's previous releases. It set the template for their "classic" sound, which is characterized by androgynous vocals, heavily distorted guitars, and extensive use of pitch bending. The EP features Shields' "glide guitar" technique, where he continuously strums the guitar while simultaneously holding the tremolo bar. He developed this technique after feeling dissatisfied with the band's conventional indie pop sound. The title track features a repetitive, aggressive riff that builds into a "holocaust" section of white noise, a feature that became a staple of their live shows. Shields stated that the song was inspired by the chaotic energy of Sonic Youth and Dinosaur Jr. The other tracks explore a more melodic approach, contrasting with the abrasive title track. "Slow" features a prominent bassline and Butcher's breathy vocals, while "Cigarello in Your Bed" is a sparse, acoustic-driven track.

Release

You Made Me Realise was released by Creation on 8 August 1988. The title track was released as a single the same day and limited to 1,000 copies, with the remaining tracks of the EP acting as its B-sides. The single charted on the UK Independent Chart. [14] Two promotional music videos were filmed for the title track and "Slow", both directed by Douglas Hart of The Jesus and Mary Chain. You Made Me Realise out of print following the release of their debut studio album, Isn't Anything, later that year. The EP was remastered for the compilation album EPs 1988–1991, which was released in 2012 [15] [16].
