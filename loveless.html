Loveless (Album)

Loveless is the second studio album by Irish rock band My Bloody Valentine. It was released on 4 November 1991 in the United Kingdom by Creation Records and in the United States by Sire Records. The album was recorded between February 1989 and September 1991, with vocalist and guitarist Kevin Shields leading sessions and co-producing with Colm Ó Cíosóig. Shields sought to achieve a particular sound for the record; he used tremolo picking, reversed reverb, and heavily obscured vocals.

The band went through 19 different studios and several engineers during the album's prolonged recording, with its production cost rumoured to have reached £250,000 (equivalent to £570,000 in 2021). Following Loveless's release, Creation Records dropped the band owing to the album's expense and the difficulty of working with Shields.

Background

My Bloody Valentine were scheduled to record at Blackwing Studios in Southwark, London for February 1989, and intended to conceptualize a more studio-based sound for their second album. Creation Records head Alan McGee said that My Bloody Valentine "initially went into the studio just to record an EP and it took five months." The band began recording later that month. Shields said that Creation believed the album could be recorded "in five days". McGee said that "When that didn't happen, I freaked out". The band relocated to the subterranean studio The Time Square in Elephant and Castle, London, but found it unusable. Shields said that "We couldn't do anything there ... It was a total disaster."

McGee allowed them to use the Elephant and Castle studio for "three or four weeks", but said the band achieved little there. Shields said that during this period they only recorded the drum track to "Soon", and that they were "essentially sitting around doing nothing".

Shields and Butcher's vocals were recorded over a period of months. Shield said that he and Butcher "were singing really quietly" and that "when you sing really quietly, your voice is very breathy and it lacks the character of your normal voice. So we just sang really quietly and we sang in a very similar register, so we sound like the same person a lot of the time." Butcher's vocals on "To Here Knows When" and "What You Want" are the only ones on the album that she sang alone. Shields sings lead vocals on "Sometimes", "Touched", and "Soon".

Shields described the vocal recording process: "We'd just get into the vocal booth, and I'd say, 'Right, I want you to sing this melody, and I want you to sing it like this.' And she'd just do it. She's got a really good voice. She's got a very soft, breathy voice. And she's very good at singing in tune." Butcher said that Shields "would just give me a melody and I'd sing it. I didn't really know what the words were. I'd just make up phonetic sounds."

Music

Loveless is a shoegaze, dream pop, noise pop, and avant-rock album. The album is characterized by its "wall of sound" production, which features dense layers of distorted, pitch-bent guitars, obscured vocals, and drum machines. Shields's guitar playing on the album is notable for his use of a technique he dubbed "glide guitar", which involves continuous use of the tremolo arm while strumming to create a pitch-bending effect. The album's production also features extensive use of reversed reverb and sampling.

Shields has said that he "wanted to make a record that sounded like a daydream." He cited the Beach Boys, Sonic Youth, and Dinosaur Jr. as influences on the album's sound. The album's lyrics are often obscured by the dense instrumentation and Shields's and Butcher's breathy vocals. Shields has said that the lyrics are "not very important" and that they are "just another instrument."

The album's sound has been described as "ethereal," "hypnotic," and "otherworldly." Loveless has been praised for its innovative use of guitar effects and its dense, layered production. The album has been cited as a major influence on the shoegaze, dream pop, and post-rock genres. Loveless has been named one of the greatest albums of all time by numerous publications.

Artwork

The album's cover art features a blurred, magenta-hued photograph of a guitar. The image was taken by photographer Angus Cameron, who also shot the cover for the band's previous EP, Glider. Shields has said that the cover art was inspired by the work of abstract expressionist painter Mark Rothko.

Tour

My Bloody Valentine toured extensively in support of Loveless. The band's live shows were notorious for their extreme volume, with the band often playing at levels exceeding 130 decibels. The band's concerts featured a section known as "the holocaust," during which the band would play a single chord at maximum volume for up to 30 minutes.

The band's live performances were often accompanied by a light show featuring strobe lights and projections. The tour was physically and mentally exhausting for the band, and contributed to their eventual hiatus. Following the tour, My Bloody Valentine signed with Island Records and began work on their third album. However, the band struggled to complete the album, and eventually disbanded in 1997.

Reception

Loveless was released to widespread critical acclaim. The album was praised for its innovative sound and production. Melody Maker named it the album of the year, while NME ranked it number two on their end-of-year list. The album was also a commercial success, reaching number 24 on the UK Albums Chart.

In the years since its release, Loveless has been recognized as a landmark album in the shoegaze and dream pop genres. The album has been cited as a major influence on a wide range of artists, including Smashing Pumpkins, Radiohead, and Slowdive. Loveless has been included in numerous "greatest albums of all time" lists.

In 2003, Rolling Stone ranked Loveless at number 219 on their list of the 500 Greatest Albums of All Time. In 2013, NME ranked the album at number 18 on their list of the 500 Greatest Albums of All Time. Pitchfork named Loveless the best album of the 1990s.
