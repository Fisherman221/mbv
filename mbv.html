m b v (Album)

m b v is the third studio album by the Irish rock band My Bloody Valentine, self-released on 2 February 2013. Produced by vocalist and guitarist Kevin Shields, m b v was the band's first full-length release of original material since Loveless (1991), appearing 22 years later.

Recording for the album began prior to My Bloody Valentine's breakup in 1997. Shields resumed recording in 2006, and the band reassembled, with further recording taking place prior to 2012. The album received critical acclaim upon release and was named among the best albums of 2013 by numerous publications, including Pitchfork, Spin, and The Wire.

Background and Recording

In 1997, My Bloody Valentine built a studio in South London and began recording a third album. Kevin Shields has stated that the band recorded an album's worth of material, which was scrapped in 1997. The band members gradually drifted apart and were rumoured to have broken up, with Shields suffering from a "meltdown" according to the music press. Shields relocated to a house in London and employed a rotating cast of musicians, including former Elastica member John Ewbank, to record hours of material. "It wasn't like a studio, it was a house," he stated. "It was literally a case of doing it in the bedroom and doing it in the front room, and doing it in the kitchen." [16]

My Bloody Valentine, Kevin Shields on center-right, Bilinda Butcher on center-left

Recording resumed in 2006, when Shields dug out the tapes from 1996 and 1997 and began mixing them and overdubbing tracks. Shields stated that the material recorded in 1996 and 1997 "was just bass, drums and guitar" and that he "wasn't happy with it at the time" but upon listening to it in 2006, "it sounded good." The band announced their reunion in 2007 and embarked on a world tour in 2008. During the tour, the band debuted a new song, "Rough Song", which was later renamed "New You" and appeared on the album. Following the tour, Shields returned to the studio to resume work on the album.

In 2011, Shields announced that the album was "finished" and would be released by the end of the year. However, the release was delayed, and Shields later admitted that he "didn't have the words or the melodies" for some of the songs. Shields completed the vocal recordings and final mixing in 2012. The album was mastered on 21 December 2012. The final album incorporates both the 1996-1997 recordings and the newer recordings, with Shields stating that the album is "a mixture of stuff that was recorded then and stuff that was recorded now."

The album was mastered on 21 December 2012 [22] (the day the Mayan calendar ends, referred to by Shields), following a process in which no digital processing was involved in the recording or mixing [23]. The album was recorded on 2-inch 24-track analog tape and mixed on a half-inch analog tape, with no computer equipment involved. [24]

Composition

m b v is a shoegaze and dream pop album that expands on the sound of Loveless. The album's first half features a sound similar to the band's earlier work, with "she found now" and "only tomorrow" featuring the band's signature "glide guitar" technique. The album's second half introduces new elements, with "is this and yes" consisting solely of a keyboard loop, vocals and percussion, and "new you" featuring a prominent bassline and a more straightforward pop structure. The album's final three tracks feature a more experimental approach, with "in another way" featuring a frantic rhythm and "nothing is" featuring a repetitive, heavy guitar riff. The album's closing track, "wonder 2", features phaser effects and frantic drum and bass-style drumming, resembling the sound of a jet engine. [25]

The album features the prominent use of pitch bend on the guitar, a technique Shields used extensively on Loveless. However, unlike Loveless, which featured primarily sampled drum beats by Colm O'Ciosoig, the drumming on m b v was entirely recorded live. Shields stated that the drum parts were "recorded mostly in one take." [26]

In 2007, Kevin Shields mentioned to the music press that the new record "sounds like what we sounded like... it's not going to sound like a 90s band." and revealed that the 1996 sessions were largely inspired by drum and bass and jungle music, but that he wasn't happy with the results. However, elements of those sessions are evident in the final 2013 version of the album, particularly in the closing track. [27]

Release

m b v was released on 2 February 2013, self-released through the band's website. The album's release was preceded by a listening party at a club in London, where the album was played in its entirety for journalists and fans. The album was released in digital format (MP3, 16-bit WAV, and 24-bit WAV), as well as on CD and 180-gram vinyl. The website crashed on the day of the release due to heavy traffic. The vinyl and CD versions were manufactured by the band and distributed independently. The physical copies of the album were packaged in a gatefold sleeve, with artwork by Kevin Shields and Matthew Cooper. The album artwork features a blurred image of the band, with the title "m b v" printed in lower-case letters. The album's release was widely anticipated, having been named one of the most anticipated albums of the year by NME.

Tour

A fraction of Kevin Shields' live rig set up at the Aragon Theatre

The band embarked on a world tour in 2013 to support the album, with shows in Europe, Japan, and North America. The tour began on 3 February 2013, with a show at the Electric Brixton in London. The band performed in South Korea and Australia for the first time in their career. The band's live shows featured a highly visual component, with elaborate light shows and projections. The tour culminated with a series of festival performances, including a headline slot at the FYF Fest in Los Angeles. During the 2013 performances, the band played three songs from the album: "new you", "only tomorrow", and "wonder 2". The band's tour concluded with a performance at the Hammerstein Ballroom in New York City in November 2013.
